Here's a time line of singer Jim Morrison's life:

  • 1943 - born in Melbourne, Florida
  • 1947 - born his younger sister Anne Robin,in Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • 1948 - born his younger brother Andrew Lee Morrison, in Los Altos, California
  • 1957 - Morrison attended Alameda High School in Alameda, California for his freshman and first semester of his sophomore year
  • 1961 - he finished high school in Alexandria, Virginia, graduating from George Washington High School (now George Washington Middle School)
  • 1962 - transferred to Florida State University (FSU) in Tallahassee, where he appeared in a school recruitment film
  • 1964 - Morrison moved to Los Angeles to attend the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA)
  • 1965 - formed The Doors an American rock band in Los Angeles, with vocalist Jim Morrison, keyboardist Ray Manzarek, guitarist Robby Krieger, and John Densmore on drums
  • 1966 - Morrison and the Doors were the opening act at the Whisky a Go Go in the last week of the residency of Van Morrison's band Them
  • 1967 - The Doors achieved national recognition after signing with Elektra Records
  • 1968 - the Doors released their third studio album, Waiting for the Sun
  • 1970 - Morrison was convicted of indecent exposure and profanity
  • 1970 - After a lengthy break, the group reconvened to record what would become their final album with Morrison, titled L.A. Woman
  • 1971 - He died at age 27
